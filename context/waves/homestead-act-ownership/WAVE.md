# Phase 4: Homestead Act - People and Ownership

## Goal

Make territory ownership operationally fair by modeling people, capacity, and
workload limits.

## Thesis

Balanced territories are not reviewable until the people assigned to them can
see capacity, workload, and ownership expectations in the same artifacts as the
geographic split.

## Pulse table

| Pulse | Title | Status | Outcome |
|------:|-------|--------|---------|
| 01 | Assignee capacity intake | done | Parse capacity, home-base, team, and skill fields for assigned people or teams. |
| 02 | Capacity flags | pending | Flag overloaded territories and assignees in audit output. |
| 03 | Ownership visual bindings | pending | Carry capacity and owner fields into SVG and GeoJSON exports. |
| 04 | Fairness review packet | pending | Package people, capacity, movement, and exception outputs for review. |

## Success criteria

- Assigned people and teams have explicit capacity context.
- Capacity exceptions are visible in audit and packet outputs.
- Visual exports keep owner and capacity bindings stable.
- Shared-kernel candidates remain separate from TERRAIN-specific policy.
